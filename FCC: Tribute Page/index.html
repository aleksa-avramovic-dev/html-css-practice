Nikola Tesla

Inventor of alternating current (AC) motor

At a laboratory in Colorado Springs, Tesla worked with a magnifying transmitter, a device he used to conduct experiments in sending electricity without wires.

  • 1859 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire
  • 1870 - Contracted cholera. He was in hospital for close to one year, and he faced near-death circumstances.
  • 1875 - Enrolled to Polytehnic College based on a miltary scholarship.
  • 1880 - Moves to Prague to study philosophy
  • 1881 - Tesla begins his career as chief electrical engineer, he moved to Budapest in Hungary.
  • 1882 - Tesla goes to work for Edison. He found another job in Paris with a company refered to as the Continental Edison Company.
  • 1884 - Tesla moved to New York, USA.
  • 1885 - Teslas submits some of his first paterns, including developing an enhanced DC motor.
  • 1887 - Tesla Re-imagines the electric motor. He came up with an induction motor that was operated by alternating current, a form of power that was becoming common in Europe and the USA.
  • 1890 - Tesla begins his work on wireless power transmission. Tesla researched various approaches to transmitting power by inductive and capacitive coupling. This was through the use of AC voltages that were produced by his famous `Tesla coil.`
  • 1891 - At the age of 35, he became a naturalized citizen of the US. In the same year, he also patented his famous Tesla Coil.
  • 1943 - In early 1943, at age 86, Tesla died in a Hotel Room in New York. He died alone, and his body was found by a maid who ignored a do not disturb sign on Tesla’s room.
